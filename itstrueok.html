How we know this

When MH370 lost connection with ATC over the South China Sea,
there were still satellite connections that remained intact. The
satellite company, Inmarsat was still tracking the flight's movements.

Once the final transmission happened, there was an "arc" that the
satellite had marked, determined by the Australian Government to
be the general area where Flight 370 crashed.

After reading this, you might think, "how do you know it was a crash?" Luckily for you, in the years of 2015 and 2016, Boeing 777-200 debris began washing up on African shores. Some of these were even confirmed to be from MH370. With this intriguing debris, and satellite data, it's safe to say this information is true.
